## About

**Barry P. Schoedel** is the founder of [Catholic.Work](Catholic-Work) and the creator of [Catholic Ride Share](About-the-App). He works at the intersection of Catholic ministry and modern technology, building digital tools and platforms that serve the Church's evangelizing and formational mission.

---

## Professional Background

### Catholic.Work — Founder & Lead Creator/Developer
*(June 2024 – present)*

Barry founded Catholic.Work as a nonprofit dedicated to building new technological roads for the Church in the digital age. As Lead Creator and Developer, he oversees the organization's vision, projects, and technical execution — including Catholic Ride Share.

### Schoedel Systems Design, LLC — Owner & Principal Consultant

Barry is the owner of Schoedel Systems Design (also operating as Schoedel.Design and OpsHub), a consulting firm offering:

- Custom application and systems development
- AI integration and AI-assisted operations
- Public-facing web platforms and internal operations tooling
- A dedicated **Apostolate Tech** service line for Catholic dioceses, parishes, charities, and schools

This firm is the commercial arm that funds and supports the nonprofit work of Catholic.Work.

### Archdiocese of San Antonio — Director of Family Evangelization and Faith Formation

Barry served in a senior leadership role focused on family evangelization and faith formation at the archdiocesan level, directing programs that helped families live out their domestic-church vocation and pass the faith to the next generation.

### Diocese of Baton Rouge — Associate Director of Evangelization and Catechesis

Barry served the Diocese of Baton Rouge in evangelization and catechesis, helping the diocese reach and form both new Catholics and lifelong believers.

## Philosophy

Barry's work is grounded in a missionary vision for the Church in the digital age. Key themes:

- **Technology in service of the Gospel** — digital tools are means, not ends; they must orient people toward the Kingdom of God.
- **Baptismal identity** — every Catholic has a missionary vocation; technology can equip and connect people to live it out.
- **Subsidiarity and communion** — solutions should serve and strengthen parishes and dioceses, not bypass or replace them.
- **Ethical AI** — AI must be implemented safely, transparently, and in ways consistent with human dignity and Church teaching.
