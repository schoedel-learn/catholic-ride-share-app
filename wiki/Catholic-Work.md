## Overview

**Catholic.Work** is a nonprofit organization dedicated to building new roads to Emmaus in the "digital city" — using contemporary digital and technological means to help Catholics grow in faith, hope, and love, and to strengthen the Church's mission in the modern world.

The organization was founded by [Barry Schoedel](Barry-Schoedel) and operates at the intersection of Catholic ministry and technological innovation, creating tools and platforms that serve families, parishes, dioceses, and the broader Catholic community.

---

## Mission

Catholic.Work's mission is rooted in the Great Commission and the call of Christ to repentance, belief, and seeking the Kingdom of God. Its work focuses on four interconnected goals:

1. **Uniting families** so that homes become "little churches" — passing on Catholic faith, values, and devotional life to children.
2. **Supporting parishes** in their integral mission: evangelizing seekers and forming them as disciples of Christ.
3. **Promoting ongoing formation** so that people grow in their specific vocations within the Church — as spouses, parents, ministers, and lay apostles.
4. **Constructing new spiritual pathways** that use technology effectively while always orienting lives toward the Kingdom of God.

Core values: **Faith, Hope, and Love**.

---

## Relationship to the Church

Catholic.Work is structured to support dioceses and parishes without being financially dependent on their resources. The organization operates under the discretion of Catholic bishops and emphasizes:

- Helping people recognize their baptismal identity and the universal priesthood of the faithful.
- Serving the hierarchical mission of the Church rather than operating independently of it.
- Keeping all digital tools anchored in sound Catholic theology and ecclesiology.

## Projects

### Catholic Ride Share *(active)*

Catholic Ride Share is the flagship open-source project of Catholic.Work — a volunteer-based ride-sharing platform that connects Catholics who need transportation to Mass, Confession, and parish events with willing volunteer drivers. It is built with a special concern for rural communities where transportation barriers are most acute.

See [About the App](About-the-App) for full details.

### Apostolate Tech *(service line via Schoedel Systems Design)*

Barry Schoedel's consulting business (Schoedel Systems Design) includes an **Apostolate Tech** branch that develops systems tailored to Catholic dioceses, parishes, charities, and schools. Services include:

- Fundraising and data management systems
- Outreach and evangelization platforms
- Safe and ethical AI implementation for Catholic institutions
- Custom applications and internal operations tooling

Catholic.Work was founded directly out of this Apostolate Tech stream, turning mission-driven consulting work into a dedicated nonprofit.
